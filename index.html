How college kids get around


If theres one thing a university student is, it’s busy. They have to go places constantly, but what makes them choose between the car or public transport? We’ve checked the data on hundreds of these university students to find out what’s the difference between a girl on the street or a woman on the train.

Transit based on location

Notes: Click the words to filter their respective mode of transport.

This data shows how many people use each transit based on their location. The type of transit used correlates strongly with how far you are from your university’s campus. Car use is consistent within all ranges, most likely due to its convenience to drive around. Trains are more frequent the further you are from school. The biggest use of buses is close to the school.
Note that walking, the train and the bus all share similar locations, suggesting that all non car transports (public transport) are taken together.


What this all means

The hundreds of surveyed university students provided great insights on how people get around. How uni students get around is impacted by their responsibilities and where they are in relation to their destination. If their daily circumstances were different, their methods of transport would surely change as well.
